Preface

In 2011 I watched Mark Sanchez out-duel the unstoppable force of Tom Brady, giving him one of his 11 playoff losses. In that moment, I gained a level of love and appreciation for the underdog story and felt like I needed to hop on that Jets bandwagon. That win was the last Jets playoff game. In the last four seasons, the team has finished with an accumulative record of 17-31. The only way how terrible 17-31 can be emphasised is by putting you in my perspective as a Jets fan. I’d like to believe that most fans look forward to seeing their football team play on the weekend like going to church. Unlike other major sports, where teams play 3-4 times a week, I have to wait a full week before I get disappointed. For over a decade, I’ve become numb to this feeling, salvaging hope that a team that has failed to reach the playoffs for over a decade might win on a weekly basis. For the last 12 years, I’ve felt the same emotion watching the Jets—until last season.
